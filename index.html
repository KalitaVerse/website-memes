Meme Origins

Internet Archaeology
2005
Keyboard Cat
Filmed in 1984 by Charlie Schmidt, "Keyboard Cat" became a viral sensation when it was posted to YouTube in 2007. The format of "playing someone off" using the clip spawned thousands of remix videos.
2010
Trollface / Rage Comics
Drawn by Carlos Ramirez in 2008, the Trollface became the cornerstone of an entire genre of comics that dominated early Reddit and 4chan culture. Simple stick figures conveying complex emotions.
2012
Gangnam Style
PSY's breakout hit was the first YouTube video to hit 1 billion views, spawning countless parodies and solidifying the concept of a "viral moment" in the social media age.
2016
This Is Fine 🔥
Originally from KC Green's webcomic "Gunshow" (2013), the dog sitting calmly in a burning room became the defining image of 2016's chaotic political climate and remains endlessly relevant.
2019
Woman Yelling At Cat
A mashup of a Real Housewives screencap and Smudge the cat, this format exploded across all platforms and became one of the most versatile reaction meme formats ever created.
2022
NPC Streaming Trend
Streamers mimicking non-player characters from video games, repeating actions and phrases in loops, became a bizarre but oddly compelling TikTok phenomenon that blurred the line between content and performance art.
